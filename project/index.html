According to a study by the National Center for Biotechnology Information, the average American eats approximately 26 unique foods over a 3 day period.
From here, we'll divide the foods by how often Americans proportionally eat breakfast, lunch, and dinner.
Next we'll remove the percentage of these meals that Americans typically eat alone.
Now we have the number of different foods the typical American eats in the presence of another person in three days.


16 unique foods

One of the predictors of Avoidant Restrictive Food Intake Disorder (ARFID) is only being comfortable eating 20 or less food groups.

Based on what we normally observe others eat, this could easily go unnoticed.
